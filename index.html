❯ Postgres or MongoDB for the new analytics service?
raw reply · prose

For the analytics service, Postgres is generally the stronger default: it gives you a mature relational model with JSONB when you need flexibility, ACID transactions with cross-row consistency, and decades of operational tooling — though it scales horizontally only with deliberate effort. MongoDB, by contrast, offers a flexible document schema and native sharding that suits write-heavy or rapidly-changing shapes, but you trade away cross-document transactional guarantees and the kind of ad-hoc SQL analytics this workload leans on. Given that the service is analytics-first, Postgres is the safer pick unless the schema is genuinely fluid.

the shift

We optimized generation.
Nobody optimized absorption.

Better models made AI write more, faster. Chat made it trivial to ask. Neither did anything about the cost of reading the reply — the brain-tax of parsing a wall of text to extract a small payload. That's the axis Skald optimizes.

today — prose by default

Skim, scroll, re-read

  • One shape — prose — for every answer, whatever it actually contains.
  • You skim the wall and hope you didn't miss the one operative line.
  • Structure that was in the content stays in your head; you scroll back to re-find it.
with Skald — shape by default

Scan, don't parse

  • Each answer is routed to its lowest-effort form — table, steps, reference, or tight prose.
  • The structure is on the page, not in your memory. You scan instead of parse.
  • Reshaped, never reduced — every fact preserved, with one-tap revert when it misses.
the shapes

Six shapes. You never pick one.

Skald routes by what the content is, not by a style you declared — style-matching is the debunked learning-styles myth. Is the order load-bearing? Steps. A choice to weigh? Comparison. No structure to extract? It stays prose.

the promise

Reshaped. Never reduced.

Summarization throws information away to save reading. Skald refuses that trade — it only changes the shape, and it checks. Every reshaped answer keeps its original source for verification and one-tap revert.

Preservation is instrumented, not assumed. A dropped nuance is a bug — so it's measured on every reshape, and the source rides along for audit. Summarization is opt-in, never the default.

under the hood

An engine, not a prompt trick.

Precision-first router

Structural detectors first, a semantic classifier second, and abstain-to-passthrough when unsure. It would rather do nothing than reshape wrongly.

Portable IR

The engine emits an intermediate representation — mode + content + source — not pre-rendered pixels. Surfaces paint it. The IR is the asset.

Two paths in

Native emission — a model emits Skald via a system prompt, no vendor needed — and post-hoc translation that parses ordinary prose, the compatibility path.

Instrumented losslessness

Preservation is verified per reshape, not assumed. The original source rides along for revert and audit — the trust signal no other AI surface makes visible.

Vendor- & surface-agnostic

A zero-DOM TypeScript engine at the output boundary. Any model behind it; web, IDE, or terminal in front — terminal degrades to ASCII.

Learning layer · secondary

Logs reading-effort signals to tune density and tie-breaks only. It never overrides a shape the content justifies.

open core

One IR. Three layers. Every surface.

The Skald Protocol — the IR as a published standard — is the open core. The Composer turns it into styled, interactive, mixed-mode layout; that's the moat. Renderers paint it per surface.

layer 01

Protocolopen core

The IR as a standard — mode, parsed content, and the original source. The portable contract every producer and surface agrees on. Published once it stabilizes against the renderer.

layer 02

Composerthe moat

IR → styled, interactive, mixed-mode layout. The mode signatures, the highlight vocabulary, the lossless affordance — where the reading quality actually lives.

layer 03

Renderers

Per-surface paint over the same IR. A web client is in development first; IDE and terminal follow — the terminal renderer degrades cleanly to ASCII.
